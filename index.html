01 — Purpose

What these
videos are for.

MISSION

Top-of-funnel awareness reels for Instagram.

Each video takes a single insight, stat, or concept and brings it to life through kinetic text and sound. No fluff, no filler, no brand theater.

ONE SHARP IDEA. NOTHING ELSE.
01 — OBJECTIVE

Stop the scroll

You have less than two seconds before someone swipes past. Every frame has to earn the next one. There is no room for a slow build.

02 — TONE

Never feel like an ad

This is a sharp person talking directly at you through moving type. Not a brand announcement. Not a thought-leader carousel. Something real.

03 — OUTCOME

Wispr Flow feels inevitable

By the end, the viewer should feel like Wispr Flow is the obvious next step — not because they were sold to, but because the idea landed.

02 — Color Palette

Two or three.
Never more.

Off-White
#F5F2EE · #FFFFFF
Primary background. The resting state. Most frames live here.
Deep Charcoal
#1A1A1A
Dark sections, all text, impact moments. The default for emphasis.
Warm Grey
#E8E4DF
Cards, secondary backgrounds. Between off-white and full dark.
Orange
#E8510A
THE accent. One word. One callout. Use it once, max twice. Lose the power if overused.
Rule: 2–3 colors per video. One dominant background · one text color · the orange hits once.

04 — Visual Style

How the
frame works.

01

Kinetic Typography

Text animates in with purpose — slams, cuts, snaps. Nothing sits still too long. Every entrance is a decision.

02

Big, Bold Type

Key words get size, weight, or color emphasis. Not everything is the same size. The hierarchy is the design.

03

Negative Space

A single word centered on a dark background hits harder than a packed frame. Empty is power.

04

Numbers Own Moments

Stats and numbers get their own frame. Big, centered, animated in with impact. Never buried in a sentence.

05

No Decoration

No gradients, no sticker emojis, no illustrated icons. Pure type on clean backgrounds. Decoration is a crutch.

06

One Idea Per Frame

Each scene carries exactly one thought. When the idea lands, cut. Never wait for comfort — cut when it's done.

05 — Motion Principles

Animation
has rules.

Sync to the voice

Animation reacts to words — when the voiceover pauses, something lands visually. The text is transcription, not decoration.

trigger on word

Entrances over exits

Spend all your energy on how things come in. Exits can be fast cuts. Nobody remembers things leaving — they remember things arriving.

exits = instant cut

Half-second ceiling

No single animation runs longer than 500ms. If it takes longer, it's too slow. Attention is expensive and you're spending it.

max 500ms

Slams, not slides

Use snap entrances: scale from 0.8→1, or translateY from +20px to 0, with ease-out. Never a gentle float. It should feel decisive.

ease-out · snap

Pauses land the punch

When the voiceover pauses, hold the frame. That silence is the moment the idea actually lands. Don't animate through it.

hold on pause

Orange moves last

The orange accent should enter after the main copy. It's the emphasis, not the lead. One orange word per beat maximum.

accent · delay 0.2s
06 — Sound Design

Sound isn't
optional.

Text entrance = sound

Every text entrance gets a sound — subtle whooshes, clicks, impacts. Never silent. The sound confirms the visual. Short, clean, intentional.

Per word

Music bed: atmosphere only

Low, instrumental, no lyrics. No busy beats. The music is the room the words live in — not the point. If you notice it, it's too loud.

Background

The last line hits differently

A drop, a single hard impact, or sudden silence. Pick one and commit. This is the moment that determines whether the video is remembered. Don't hedge it.

Ending
07 — Do & Don't

Hard rules.
No exceptions.

✓ Do
Pure motion graphics — kinetic type, clean frames, intentional animation
One dominant background color, one text color, one orange accent hit per video
Arial Bold Condensed for all caption/spoken text — mirrors the voiceover exactly
Numbers get their own full frame — big, centered, animated in with impact
End with energy — a drop, an impact, or deliberate silence. Commit to it.
Animate entrances. Cut exits. All budget goes to arrivals.
Sync every animation to the voiceover beat — word triggers animation
✕ Don't
B-roll, stock footage, or talking head clips — this is not a hybrid video
Slide transitions that feel like PowerPoint — no push, no wipe, no zoom-in-from-everywhere
Gradients that look like a 2019 SaaS landing page — no purple-to-blue, no anything-to-anything
Animated emoji or sticker-style graphics — ever
More than 3 fonts — max is max
Ending on a static logo with no energy — the last frame must land, not coast
Animation longer than 500ms — if it takes that long it's slow and soft
GUT CHECK
08 — The Final Test
If it feels like an ad or a thought leader slideshow, it's wrong.
If it feels like a sharp person talking directly at you through moving text, it's right.
